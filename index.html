10 interesting facts

  • The human body contains 30 000 billion red blood cells.
  • human body is estimated to have 60,000 miles of blood vessels.
  • human heart beats 30 million times a year.
  • Every square inch of the human body has about 19 million skin cells.
  • Every hour about 1 billion cells in the human body must be replaced.
  • The adult body is made up of: 100 trillion cells, 206 bones, 600 muscles, and 22 internal organs.
  • Humans have a diving reflex that shuts down bodily functions when submerged in water to prevent drowning.
  • In cases of extreme starvation, the brain will begin to eat itself.
  • Your ears and your nose never stop growing.
  • The average person produces enough saliva in their lifetime to fill two swimming pools.

Human Brain

  • The human brain is the largest brain of all vertebrates relative to body size.
  • It weighs about 3.3 lbs. (1.5 kilograms).
  • The average male has a brain volume of 1,274 cubic centimeters (cm3).
  • The average female brain has a volume of 1,131 cm3.
  • The brain makes up about 2 percent of a human's body weight.
  • It contains about 86 billion nerve cells (neurons) - the "gray matter."
